Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about switching to Sunara Energy.

Common Questions

How do I switch to Sunara Energy?+
Switching is simple. Enter your ZIP code to see available plans, choose the one that fits your budget, and complete the short enrollment form. We handle the rest — including notifying your current provider. Most customers are switched within 1–2 billing cycles.
Do I need a deposit to sign up?+
Many of our plans require no deposit at all. Deposit requirements depend on the plan selected and a quick credit check. Our Flex Plan is available with no deposit and no credit check for most customers.
Can I get same-day electricity service?+
Yes! If you enroll before 12pm on a business day, we can often activate your service the same day. Enrollment after noon is typically activated the next business day. We will always confirm your start date after you apply.
What is the difference between prepaid and postpaid plans?+
Postpaid plans bill you at the end of each month based on your actual usage. Prepaid plans let you pay upfront and use electricity as you go — no deposit required and no credit check. Both options are available at Sunara Energy.
Are there any hidden fees?+
No. Sunara Energy does not charge enrollment fees, setup fees, or surprise charges. Your rate is exactly what you see on the plan page. The only additional items on your bill are standard TDU pass-through charges set by the grid operator — not us.
What happens if I cancel my contract early?+
The Flex Plan has no contract and no cancellation fee — cancel anytime. The 12-Month Saver has a $150 early termination fee, and the 24-Month Ultra has a $250 fee. These fees do not apply if you move out of your service address.
How do I pay my bill?+
You can pay online through your account portal, by phone, or by setting up autopay. We accept all major credit cards, debit cards, and ACH bank transfers. Autopay customers receive a $5/month discount.
What if there is a power outage?+
Power outages are handled by your local TDU (like Oncor or CenterPoint), not your retail electricity provider. Call your TDU directly to report outages. We can provide the right outage number for your area — just contact our support team.
Is Sunara Energy available in my area?+
Sunara Energy serves deregulated electricity areas in Texas. Enter your ZIP code on our homepage or plans page to instantly check availability in your area.
How do I contact customer support?+
You can reach us by phone at (800) 555-1234, by email through our contact page, or through your online account portal. Our support team is available Mon–Fri 8am–8pm and Saturday 9am–5pm.
